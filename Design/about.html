About Sixten Ehrling

Sixten Ehrling (1918-2005) was a distinguished Swedish conductor, known for his versatility and precision. He began his career as a pianist but soon transitioned to conducting, studying under prominent figures like Tor Mann and Herbert von Karajan. As the chief conductor of the Royal Swedish Opera (1953-1960), he led groundbreaking productions and gained international recognition.

Ehrling later moved to the United States, where he conducted the Detroit Symphony Orchestra for many years, contributing to the orchestra's growing global reputation. He also collaborated with renowned orchestras such as the New York Philharmonic and the Metropolitan Opera. Ehrling passed away in New York in 2005, and his legacy as one of the most influential Swedish conductors of the 20th century endures.
